The part of the site that does not blink. The why behind the where.

The history we are walking in

Stonewall.

June 28, 1969. Police raided the Stonewall Inn in Greenwich Village. The patrons fought back. The uprising lasted days. The first Christopher Street Liberation Day March was held one year later, June 1970. That march became Pride.

The "Mother of Pride" was Brenda Howard. A bisexual feminist organizer who came up with the idea of a week-long event. The week grew into a month. Bill Clinton officially declared June as Pride Month in 1999.

The history of this month is the history of riots-becoming-marches-becoming-a-claim. The right to be seen, named, and unbothered.

If we walk in a parade this month, we are walking in that. Not next to it. In it.

2026 specifically

The climate.

This year is rougher. I can say that out loud.

Federal rollback. The Trump administration has actively pursued the dismantling of LGBTQ+ protections, particularly for trans people. Congressional Equality Caucus calls for federal action are louder. Hard-fought freedoms are being walked backwards in real time.

Corporate pullback. Sponsors who painted rainbows for a decade have quietly stepped back. Some are still funding privately. Most are just gone from the photo. The 2026 parades have less corporate signage and more handmade signs. Honestly, this might be a feature.

Activists at the front of the march. The community is louder, the brands are quieter, the moment is sharper. This is Pride returning to the protest it always was.

For All of Us. Pride 2026 theme · Stonewall UK + the Congressional Equality Caucus

The theme is not abstract. It is for the trans community whose right to exist is being weaponized into a culture war. It is for the queer kids in red states whose schools are stripping their books. It is for the elders who marched in 1970 and are still marching in 2026.

It is for the friends I am gathering this month, and the ones I am not.

The work

Gay Gaming Professionals.

GGP is the 501(c)(3) I founded for lesbian, gay, bisexual, transgender and queer game industry professionals and enthusiasts around the world. The mission is education, expertise, employment, and entrepreneurship.

Joe is the CFO. Eric is the EP. Mabel and Alex are on the team. Mychal is in the orbit. Sinjin Bain is our anchor sponsor partner for the Big Beautiful Bill Challenge in 2026.

The work in 2026 is more important than the marketing of the work. Quiet money is louder than loud logos this year. Scholarships, not sponsorships, are the pitch I am making.

If you are reading this and you want to support GGP this Pride Month, the move is the GGP Scholars program. Reach out to me directly.

What June actually means here

Three things this month is for.

1. Showing up in person. The 2026 Pride is more about who is in the room with you and less about who is paying for the float. Pink Pony Rodeo with Joe at the LA LGBT Center on June 5. Gayming Awards with the crew of six on June 8. LA Pride Sunday June 14. Chicago Pride Sunday June 28 with Eric and Crystal. Four parades and adjacent events on my calendar.

2. Naming the chosen family. The circle is wide. Drake and Mel. Quinn and Caryn. Mason and Mychal. Eric and Crystal. Joe. Brennan. Foster. Pete. Day 1s and kin and the ones who have been showing up since 2016. They are family. Pride is for the people who walk with us. That is what "For All of Us" actually means.

3. Eric's first ever Pride. June 28 in Chicago. My ally-EP of GGP at his first parade in his life. This is the closing keystone. Of every event on the June calendar, this is the one that I am most committed to being present for. The first time a friend walks in a parade is sacred.

Pride is a march. Not a logo. Gordon · June 2026
If you want to do something

Five small moves.

Pride in a hostile climate is not about big posts. It is about small, consistent moves. Five I would recommend if you are reading this and want to act:

  • 1. Walk in a parade in your city. Just once. With a friend.
  • 2. Send money to a community-funded LGBTQ+ org in your state. Not the national brand. The local one.
  • 3. Vote like rights are on the line. They are.
  • 4. Sponsor a GGP Scholar if you are in the gaming industry. Reach out to me.
  • 5. Show up for your queer friends in private. Not in performance.
