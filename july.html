The first time

Leavenworth. Officiating Dane and Megan.

Saturday July 25, 2026. Leavenworth, Washington. A Bavarian-themed town in the Cascade Mountains, two hours east of Seattle.

I am officiating Dane Lennon and Megan Lennon's wedding.

This is the first time in my life I have had this honor and privilege.

Dane is one of the people who got me into Shambhala. He and Drake are the two co-evangelists of the festival that changed my last five years. The trade I am making this year is to skip Shambhala in order to stand for Dane on the day he marries the love of his life. The friend who brought me to the music is the friend whose marriage I am standing up for. Same weekend the festival would have been mine. Mine is here instead.

Megan is Dane's partner in everything that comes next. I have only met her a handful of times in person. By the time I am standing in front of them in Leavenworth in late July, I will have spent a month of quiet daily preparation getting to know who they are to each other.

This is my first wedding as an officiant. It comes after my first wedding as an emcee (Drake and Mel, May 30). Two weddings, two friends-as-grooms, two roles I have never held before. 2026 is the year I learned how to stand for the people I love at the front of a room.
